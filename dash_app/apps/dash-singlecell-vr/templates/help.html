singlecellVR Help

What is singlecellVR

singlecellVR is a browser-contained, free, and open-access tool that allows users to interactively explore published studies or new data through an innovative VR interface. singlecellVR supports clustering, trajectory inference and abstract graph analysis for transcriptomic as well as epigenomic single cell data. We have developed a one-command conversion tool called scvr to make the results of commonly-used single-cell analysis tools compatible with singlecellVR.




What can I do with singlecellVR?

singlecellVR can be used to visualize the output of commonly-used single cell analysis tools, including Scanpy/epiScanpy, Seurat, PAGA, and STREAM. singlecellVR also supports multiple cell annotations (e.g. FACS-sorting labels, clustering solutions, pseudotime, etc.), and feature quantification (gene expression levels, transcription factor deviation, etc.).

(1) Scanpy and epiScanpy

    You can visualize and explore the cellular clusters and gene expression/open chromatin/DNA methylation patterns at single-cell level https://scanpy.readthedocs.io.

(2) Seruat:

    You can visualize and explore the cellular clusters and gene expression/open chromatin patterns at single-cell level https://satijalab.org/seurat.

(3) PAGA graph:

    You can visualize and explore the coarse-grained connectivity structures of cellular complex manifolds, cell states and their gene expression patterns at single-cell level https://github.com/theislab/paga.

(4) STREAM trajectories:

    You can visualize and explore the cellular developmental trajectories, cell states, and their gene expression/open chromatin/protein patterns at single-cell level https://github.com/pinellolab/STREAM.

How can I use it?

singlecellVR is a web tool for single-cell data exploration. VR Headset device required. Google Cardboard is currently the primarily supported headset for this data visualization software.

What type of hardware do I need?

There are several similar versions of a cardboard adapter available for ~$8. This can be purchased on websites like Amazon.com or Google. You can also simply use your computer screen to do initial exploration of the data in 2-D. It is advisable to use a keyboard to unlock the full set of interactive functionalities associated with these key shortcuts:

  • Space bar: Summons the menu in front of the camera
  • Control-Space bar: Sends the menu to its starting position
  • Shift (mobile only): Summons the minimap
  • Alphanumeric keys: Allow user to search for genes to display
  • Enter: Resets cell colors and the search results
  • Up and down arrows: Move user in direction or opposite direction of where they are looking
  • Control-Plus and Control-Minus: Occasionally, cells may appear too large or too small and may interfere with the visualization. In such a case, Control-plus will increase the size of the cells and Control-minus will decrease the size of the cells.



You can easily connect a keyboard with your smartphone using a bluetooth enabled keyboard (a small portable keyboard can be purchased from Amazon for ~$8). However, you can still use a normal computer and your browser and explore with your mouse and keyboard the 3d transcriptional space with cells, trajectories and the graph abstraction.

Many VR headsets such as Google Cardboard have a single button that allows a user to click the screen of their phone while immersed in a virtual reality experience. By holding down this button, users without a keyboard may move forward in the direction of their gaze. Additionally, users may select the "keyboard" button on the menu to render a virtual keyboard. Cardboard users may use this keyboard to search for available features to render on the display. The "Enter/Return" key on the virtual keyboard clears the current search. Subsequently selecting the "keyboard" button will hide the keyboard from view.
