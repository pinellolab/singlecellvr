Single Cell VR Help

What is Single Cell VR

SingleCellVR is a website that allows users to interactively explore published studies or new data through an innovative VR interface. In a 3D space, users can explore developmental trajectories, cellular composition, and stable cell states. In addition, cells can be colored based on metadata such as cell type, respective clustering, and gene expression.




What can I do with Single Cell VR?

SingleCellVR can be used to visualize two types of analysis results on transcriptomic data:

(1) STREAM trajectories:

    You can visualize and explore the cellular developmental trajectories, cell states, and their gene expression patterns at single-cell level https://github.com/pinellolab/STREAM.

(2) PAGA graph:

    You can visualize and explore the coarse-grained connectivity structures of cellular complex manifolds, cell states and their gene expression patterns at single-cell level https://github.com/theislab/paga.

How can I use it?

Single Cell Virtual Reality is a web tool for single-cell data exploration. VR Headset device required. Google Cardboard is currently the primarily supported headset for this data visualization software.

What type of hardware do I need?

There are several similar versions of a cardboard adapter available for ~$15. This can be purchased on websites like Amazon.com or Google. You can also simply use your computer screen to do initial exploration of the data in 2-D. It is advisable to use a keyboard to unlock the full set of interactive functionalities associated with these key shortcuts:

  • Space bar: Summons the menu in front of the camera
  • Control-Space bar: Sends the menu to its starting position
  • Shift (mobile only): Summons the minimap
  • Alphanumeric keys: Allow user to search for genes to display
  • Enter: Resets cell colors and the search results
  • Up and down arrows: Move user in direction or opposite direction of where they are looking
  • Control-Plus and Control-Minus: Occasionally, cells may appear too large or too small and may interfere with the visualization. In such a case, Control-plus will increase the size of the cells and Control-minus will decrease the size of the cells.

You can easily connect a keyboard with your smartphone using a bluetooth enabled keyboard (a small portable keyboard can be purchased from Amazon for ~$15).However, you can still use a normal computer and your browser and explore with your mouse and keyboard the 3d transcriptional space with cells, trajectories and the graph abstraction.
